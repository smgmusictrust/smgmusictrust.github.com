Brief Life Sketch of Smt.Mangalam Ganapathy

Smt.Mangalam Ganapathy was born in 1934 in an orthodox middle class family at Kallidaikurichi, in Tirunelveli district among seven children. Her father Sri.M.S.Subramania Iyer was a teacher in Thilakar Vidyalayam High School. He was also a writer of textbooks in Science & Social Studies. Besides he translated Tolstoy’s stories into Tamil which were prescribed as a non-detailed study for school children.

Dr.V.V.Srivatsa, Musicologist, Composer and Musician says

An in depth perusal of the compositions of Smt. Mangalam Ganapathy left an indelible impression on me and I have no hesitation whatsoever in concluding that her compositions deserve to be classified amongst the finest ones of the present era. Having heard her render some compositions, she is indeed a vaggeyakara of a unique genre.

During her childhood, she did not have any formal education in carnatic music. Tamil was her mother tongue. She learnt from her father devotional hymns and Sanskrit slokas. But she would always be listening to her elder sisters singing who were learning music from Vidwan Sri Appaiah Bhagavathar, a disciple of Sri Muthiah Bhagavathar of Harikesanallur.  She got married in the year 1954 and moved to Tiruvananthapuram where she stayed for almost 45 years. She picked up a working knowledge of Malayalam during her stay there. Household chores and family responsibilities consumed most of her time.

It all happened while in Madras in August 1998 when they were about to perform Ganapthy pooja and Devi pooja at their residence. Suddenly she started feeling the presence of elevated thoughts and in that sublime mood she composed a song “Mahaganapathim Upasmahe” in Nattaikurinji on Lord Ganesha and followed it up by another song “Amba Krupasagari” in Kalyani on Devi. She does not know how it happened but the songs were rendered with appropriate tala.

She has since then composed more than 6000 songs in three and a half years and that too in five different languages like Sanskrit, Tamil, Malayalam, Hindi and Telugu. It is only divine grace that she is able to write in languages other than Tamil and Malayalam because she never had any chance to learn them. She has written from Varnams (both thana and padha), Keerthanas, Ragamalika, Thillana and devotional bhajans. She has sung on all Hindu deities that too exceeding 100s on each deity and their incarnations and also a few philosophical and patriotic compositions too. In fact a good musician can perform a full concert from Varnam to Mangalam from her compositions with a variety to choose from raga, tala, the genre, the deity and the language. What do we call this other than a divine musical miracle?

Sangitha Kala Acharya T.S.Parthasarathy says

While editing her songs I was amazed at their simplicity, spontaneity, devotional fervour and diction. An ordinary housewife, aged 64, suddenly bursts forth into song and not only krithis, but also varnams, ragamalikas and thillanas come gushing out in hundreds. What is mind-boggling is that she has had no formal training in music and has never heard the ragas in which she was composing. More intriguing is the fact that she composes songs in languages like Telugu and Hindi with which she is not acquainted.

With encouragement from musicians and musicologists of eminence, like Sri Semmangudi Srinivasa Iyer, Smt.D.K.Pattammal, Sri A.S.Panchapakesa Iyer, Smt.Mani Krishnaswamy, Smt.Rukmini Ramani, Dr.V.V.Srivatsa and Kallidaikurichi Mahadeva Bhagavathar, two books titled “Mangala Geetham Vol.I & II containing 81 keerthanas and an audio cassette covering 12 songs were released in October 1999. It must be mentioned of the great help given by Dr.Rukmini Ramani and Sri A.S Panchapakesa Iyer in notating the songs, as Mangalam does not have any formal education in music. 

Besides the above, other luminaries of carnatic music like Sri.T.R.Subramaniam, Sri.T.K.GovindaRao, Sri.T.S.Parthasarathy, Sri.K.S.Mahadevan, Sri.Lalgudi.G.Jayaraman, Smt.R.Vedavalli, Sri.P.S.Narayanaswamy, Sri.R.Krishnaswamy, Sri.S.R.Janakiraman, Sri.Ramachandriah, Sri Vaidyanatha Krishnan, Sri.Sivaramakrishnan, Sri.O.S.Thyagarajan, Sikkil sisters and Dr.Prameela Gurumurthy have written forewords to the five volumes of books so far released.

And on 31st May 2002, a book, the third volume of “Mangala Geetham” containing 101 songs notated by Dr.Mrs.Rukmini Ramani along with the second and third volume of audio cassettes rendered by Mambalam sisters were released at a function held at Asthika Samajam, Alwarpet.  Since then another two volumes of book being Volume 4 & 5 each containing 101 songs and 7 audio cassettes and compact discs have been released by the Trust formed in her name. For details, please refer to the activities of the trust.

The composer was invited as a distinguished participant at the event “Stree Gaanam - Women composers in Carnatic Music of past and present” meet held on the eve of the International women’s day at Mumbai on 7th March 2005 at the Mysore Association Auditorium. She also participated as an invited guest at the event “Crème of 20th Century Composers Music festival” held on 10th April 2005 by the Music Education Trust, New Delhi

As part of the activities of the trust, for the propagation of her compositions, music teachers are encouraged to teach them to their pupils and the trust provides the teachers with books and audio cassettes free of cost. At frequent intervals, an opportunity is offered to the teachers to present a programme of these compositions through their students. During July 2006, Smt.Chthra Madhavan, one of the music teachers presented a programme titled “Sata Mangala Lahari”  where her students rendered 101 compositions.  Now having seen this programme, many of the teachers want to do it on an annual basis.

Sri Rama Seva mandali, Bangalore conferred a title “Gaana Kavitha Saraswathy” on Smt.Mangalam Ganapathy at a function held at Indian Heritage Association Hall, Koramangala on 16th August 2003.
