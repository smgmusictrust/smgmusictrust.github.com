About Smt.Mangalam Ganapathy Music Trust

Smt.Mangalam Ganapathy Music Trust (M.G.M.T) was founded on 22 nd July, 2002 with objectives linked to Music, Dance, Fine Arts and cultural events and activities. It is a non-profit organisation dedicated to propagate and popularize compositions of various composers in general and those of a prodigious and divine composer Smt.Mangalam Ganapathy, in particular.

The Compositions are perceptible and aesthetic. Prasam and yati prasam are handled well in the compositions. The ragas chosen are not only traditional but also suitable to the mood of the compositions. I am sure the compositions will withstand test of time.

It is nothing short of a miracle that Smt.Mangalam Ganapathy belonging to an average middle class family background with no formal association with any form of music or dance began composing songs on various deities during August 1998 at an unusual age of 64 years. In nine years of time since then, she has literally poured out more than six thousand compositions on all accepted deities in many languages that are known as well as unknown to her. She has to her credit, compositions in Tamil, Sanskrit, Malayalam, Telugu and Hindi.

Most of the acknowledged experts and doyens in the field of carnatic music like  Sri Semmangudi Srinivasa Iyer, Smt.Mani Krishnaswamy, Smt.D.K.Pattammal, Sri.T.S.Parthasarathy, Sri.T.K.Govindarao, Sri.K.S.Mahadevan, Sri.Lalgudi Jayaraman, Smt.R.Vedavalli, Sri.T.R.Subramanian, Sri.S.R.Janakiraman, Dr.V.V.Srivatsa, Sri.A.S.Panchapakesa Iyer, Smt.Rukmini Ramani, Dr.Pramila Gurumurthy and many more   have analysed, studied and concluded that “Divine Grace” has enabled this unassuming lady to write so many quality compositions in almost all known ragas & talas with such alarming speed.

Popular artistes like Bombay Sisters Smt.Saroja and Smt.Lalitha, Sri.O.S.Thyagarajan, Smt Nithyasree Mahadevan,  Sri.T.M.Krishna, Maharajapuram Sri Ramachandran, Sri Sankaran Namboodri Smt.Gayathri Girish, Smt.Geetha Raja, Smt.Vijayalakshmi Subrmaniam, Smt.lakshmi Rangarajan Mambalam sisters, Sathur sisters, Chinmaya Sisters, Sri Sreyas Narayanan, Sri.G.Srikanth, Ragam sisters, Sikkil Gurucharan, Padma Seshadri Sisters, Balamuralikrishna (junior),Dr.R.Ganesh, Bangalore Shanker and a host of other artists have performed exclusive concerts of Smt.Mangalam Ganapathy’s compositions for the trust with great devotion and aplomb lending variety, grace and frame to the wonderful songs  while also including her compositions in their concerts elsewhere. Smt.Sudha Ragunathan, Smt.Gayathri Venkatraghavan and many others have started including her compositions in their concerts from time to time. Her compositions have been adopted for Bharathanatyam also in recent programmes.

The trustees of this music trust are proud to be associated with such a good cause as spreading the immeasurable and soulful music compositions and traditions, to enable future generations to enjoy and preserve these rare happenings.

The trust holds a music competition in Chennai annually in the month of January for children of different age groups where more than 200 students participate and the best senior winner is awarded with a Thambura. It is our intention to extend this annual competition to other cities too. Up to January 2007, four such annual competitions have been held with a participation of more than 700 students. The Sangeetha Guru whose student has won the first prize in each level and the Guru who has sent the largest numbers to participate in the competition are also honoured

The trust holds concerts every two months for two to three days in Chennai and opportunities are given to students for group singing, one and a half hour concerts for budding musicians and a full time concert for senior musicians besides a programme for Bharathanatyam once in a while. The Trust since it’s inception has organized and associated in many concerts in many cities of India like Bangalore, Coimbatore, Trivandrum, Palakad and Delhi besides Chennai and these efforts will continue in order to spread and popularise the compositions to all parts of India. From April 2007, the Trust is organizing a monthly concert of Smt.Mangalam’s compositions, by a budding musician. A series of concerts including a Harikatha and a Bharathanatyam programme based on the compositions of Smt.Mangalam Ganapathy will be held from 25 th to 27 th July 2007.

The trust, on its own and with help of sponsors, has released 5 volumes of books of her compositions with notations for 380 songs  and 10 volumes of audio cassettes / compact discs rendered by popular artists. It is proposed to release one Compact disc titled “Nithya Mangala Lahari” and another titled “Swabhava Madhura Sri Madhurakali” on 14 th June 2007 at a function to be held at Bharathiya Vidya Bhavan, Mylapore, Chennai. Both are rendered by Smt.Nithyasree Mahadevan, the popular Carnatic singer. It will be followed by her concert.

Another compact disc will be released in July which will have the performance of Sathur sisters. A book of Mangala Geetham volume number 6 containing new compositions with notations will be ready for release in July. The trust plans to do these activities on an annual basis.

To continue with our efforts to popularise the compositions, the trustees, have planned many projects (for the near future and some on a continuous basis). It goes without saying that the Trust needs funds to implement these projects and looks for sponsorships and financial support from generous music lovers.

Music and other forms of arts transcend all barriers of territory and language and have universal appeal. It is our earnest appeal to all music and culture oriented persons and organisations to contribute their mite and join in this public cause.
